US Embassy to Lease KSh 4.3 Billion 90-Unit Gated Estate for Staff, Project to be Completed in 2022

  • The United States Embassy will spend KSh 4.3 billion to lease a 90-unit gated estate in Rosslyn, Gigiri, for its workers
  • Gateway Real Estate Africa Limited, a Mauritius-based real estate firm, has been contracted to undertake the project
  • The project is estimated to create 1,100 direct jobs and 500 indirect jobs and bolster the bilateral friendship between the United States and Kenya and strengthen diplomacy in the region

The United States Embassy will lease a 90-unit gated estate in Rosslyn, Gigiri, comprising apartments and townhouses dubbed ‘Rosslyn Grove for its staff.

The embassy will lease the development for eight years at the cost of KSh 4.3 billion, with the estimated first year’s rental income being KSh 533.8 million.

Rosslyn Grove will be completed by the third quarter of 2022 and has a total development cost of KSh 5.5 billion. Gateway Real Estate Africa Limited, a Mauritius-based real estate firm, is undertaking the project.

Safe living environment

According to the Embassy, the housing complex will allow shorter commutes and a safer living environment for the U.S. embassy community in Nairobi.

The construction company, the embassy said, will buy all building materials in Kenya to contribute to the local economy.

The project is estimated to create 1,100 direct jobs and 500 indirect jobs, bolster the bilateral friendship between the United States and Kenya, and strengthen diplomacy in the region.

Attractive development area

An analysis by Cytonn Investments ranks Rosslyn as an attractive residential development area supported by various factors. For instance, the categorisation of the area as a Blue Diplomatic Zone.

The area is also close to social amenities such as the Rosslyn Riviera Mall, Two Rivers Mall, and Village Market. It is also highly accessible, as Limuru Road and Kiambu Road serve it.

According to the Cytonn Annual Markets Review-2021, Rosslyn was the best performing node in the high-end segment of detached units recording an average rental yield and capital appreciation of 4.7% and 2.0%, which is 0.8% and 1.0% points higher than the market average of 3.9% and 1.0%.

“We expect the residential sector to record increased activities supported by the continued investor focus on student housing, and the uptake of residential units sparking hopes to investors,” Cytonn said in the analysis.

Who is Standup Comedian Sal Vulcano?

Salvatore Edward Anthony Vulcano, popularly known as “Sal”, is an improvisational and standup comedian, actor, and producer from Staten Island in New York City. He is a member of the comic ensemble The Tenderloins, including Joe Gatto, James Murray, and Brian Quinn.

Including these three members of The Tenderloins, the stars in the on-camera television show called Impractical Jokers. The show aired on December 15, 2011, on the TruTV television network and has been going strong since. Vulcano is the youngest member of the group.

Biography TimeLine

Sal Vulcano is a well-known American comedian, actor, and producer who rose to fame as one of the members of the comedy troupe “The Tenderloins.” He is best known for his role in the hit truTV series “Impractical Jokers,” which premiered in 2011. Over the years, Vulcano has become a prominent figure in the entertainment industry, showcasing his talents in various shows and movies. Here is a detailed biography timeline of his life and career.

  • November 6, 1976: Sal Vulcano was born in Staten Island, New York City.
  • 1994: Vulcano graduated from Monsignor Farrell High School in Staten Island.
  • 1998: He graduated from St. John’s University with a degree in Finance.
  • 1999-2000: Vulcano worked briefly in Finance before quitting to pursue a comedy career.
  • 2006: He co-founded the comedy troupe “The Tenderloins” with his high school friends Joe Gatto, James Murray, and Brian Quinn.
  • 2011: The Tenderloins’ hit TV series “Impractical Jokers” premiered on truTV, featuring Vulcano and his friends pranking each other in public.
  • 2013: Vulcano appeared in the TV series “Jokers After Party” as himself.
  • 2014: He appeared in the TV series “12 Angry Mascots” as himself.
  • 2015: Vulcano appeared in the TV series “The Misery Index” as a co-host alongside Jameela Jamil and Brian Quinn.
  • 2017: He made his acting debut in the comedy-drama film “The Big Sick” as a character named Sal.
  • 2018: Vulcano appeared as himself in the TV series “What Say You?”.
  • 2019: He voiced the character of Mr. McSweeney in the animated TV series “Crank Yankers.”
  • 2020: Vulcano released his debut comedy special, “Sal Vulcano: Live from Staten Island”, on Comedy Central.
  • 2021: He co-produced and starred in the movie “Impractical Jokers: The Movie 2.”

Early Life and Education

Vulcano was born on Staten Island and of Italian, Cuban, and Puerto Rican heritage. He went to Monsignor Farrell High School and was a member of the Improvisation Club with Gatto, Murray, and Quinn since they regarded it as a means to express themselves and meet ladies.

In 1998, he graduated from St. John’s University with a bachelor’s degree in Finance. He went to Monsignor Farrell High School, where he graduated in 1994.

Professional Career

Murray, Gatto, and Vulcano reconnected after graduating from college. He resumed practising improvisation in Gatto’s house, eventually visiting as an improv and sketch comedy team naming themselves the Tenderloins in 1999. The Tenderloins began collaborating on comedic skits, which they shared on YouTube, MySpace, and Metacafe, garnering millions of views. For the performance “Time Thugs”, the group won the $100,000 big prize in the NBC It Was Your Show competition 2007.

His most popular show, Impractical Jokers, aired on TruTV on December 15, 2011, and its first season drew over 32 million views. The show has become TruTV’s most popular series, putting Vulcano into the spotlight. Sal was the show’s most punished joker in November 2017. Additionally, the show named The Misery Index, presented by Jameela Jamil and based on Andy Breckman’s card game “Shit Happens”, featured Vulcano and the other members of The Tenderloins in 2019. The show was active for three years till 2021.

He hosted a podcast called What Say You? in collaboration with his best friend, Brian Quinn. “What Say You?” a Vulcano and Quinn-hosted infrequent podcast, has already won Best New Show at the 2013 Stitcher Awards. Because of the growing success of What Say You, Joe Gatto and James Murray decided to start their own Tenderloins podcast without the other two members. At the 10th Annual Podcast Awards 2015,” What Say You?” was again nominated for the Comedy, Entertainment, and Best Produced Podcast Awards. The podcast is their side project, not replacing The Tenderloins Podcast.

Okieriete Onaodowan is an actor and singer from the United States. Okieriete Onaodowan is popular for playing the dual roles of Hercules Mulligan and James Madison in the musical named “Hamilton” (2015).

Early Life

Okieriete Onaodowan was born on the 16th of August 1987, in Newark, New Jersey, the United States of America. This 36-year-old man has a birth sign of Leo and holds American nationality. Meanwhile, he belongs to a mixed ethnicity while his religious belief is unknown.

He was born to Nigerian parents and grew up with his five sisters, raised in West Orange, New Jersey. There is very little known about his parents and other family members in public. He may disclose about his family members in the following days.

Okieriete Onaodowan – Education

He graduated from West Orange High School in 2005 after attending Gregory Elementary School, Roosevelt Middle School, and Roosevelt High School. Additionally, he played football for his high school for a short time before retiring due to an injury suffered during practice.

As a result of his injury, he began to consider acting as an extracurricular activity. He began performing with the New Jersey Youth Theatre while in high school. After graduating from high school, he auditioned for and was accepted to Rutgers University’s Mason Gross School of the Arts. He only stayed for a year.

Professional Career

Okieriete Onaodowan gained recognition for his role as Hercules Mulligan and James Madison in the 2015 Broadway musical “Hamilton.” His powerful and energetic performance in the show earned him critical acclaim and a dedicated fan following. He was part of the original cast and appeared in the Off-Broadway production as well before the show transferred to Broadway. Later, it was converted into a movie with the same name and Onaodowan reprised the same role in the movie which was released in 2020.

Caption: Okieriete Onaodowan in the dual roles of Hercules Mulligan and James Madison in the musical, Hamilton (Source: NJ.com)

Before his breakthrough in “Hamilton,” he had already made a name for himself in the theater world. He appeared in various regional productions and Off-Broadway shows, showcasing his versatile acting and singing abilities. Some of his notable stage credits include “Rocky” on Broadway, “Bonnie & Clyde” at the Asolo Repertory Theatre, and “Luce” at Lincoln Center Theater.

In addition to his work on stage, Onaodowan has also ventured into television and film. He has appeared in TV shows such as “Gravity” (2010), “NYC 22” (2012), “Blue Bloods” (2014), “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit” (2016), and “The Get Down” (2017).  Meanwhile, he had a recurring role in the series “Grey’s Anatomy” playing the role of firefighter Dean Miller from 2018-2021. Later, he reprised the same role in the series’ second spin-off named “Station 19” from 2018-2021 appearing in 59 episodes.

On the big screen, he appeared in movies such as “Thanks for Sharing” (2012), “The Super” (2017), “A Quiet Place Part II” and “Molli and Max in the Future” (2023). He will co-star with Jessica Chastain in a revival of “A Doll’s House” directed by Jamie Lloyd and adapted by Amy Herzog in 2023. The play has a strict 16-week run that concludes in June 2023.

Okieriete Onaodowan – Awards

During his acting career, Onaodowan has won a couple of awards and nominations. Back in 20216, he won the Grammy Award-Best Musical Theater Album, for his works in the 2015 musical “Hamilton.” The same year, he was nominated for Broadway.com Audience Choice Award-Favorite Breakthrough Performance for his work in “Hamilton.” Moreover, he was included in the Forbes 30 Under 30 Hollywood & Entertainment list in 2016 for his acting in “Hamilton.”

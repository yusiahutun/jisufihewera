Justin Bieber gatecrashes Drake Bell's album launch party

Payback! Justin Bieber's Twitter feud with Drake Bell comes to a head as he prompts mass hysteria from fans by gatecrashing album launch party

A prolonged Twitter feud reportedly came to a head on Thursday when Justin Bieber made an uninvited appearance at former child star Drake Bell’s album launch in Los Angeles.

Bell was centre stage at popular cocktail bar Mixology as he celebrated the imminent release of third LP Ready, Steady, Go!

But his thunder was stolen by the presence of Bieber, who was thought to be responding to a series of derisory tweets from the singer and actor by casually passing the venue in blacked out sprinter van – prompting mass hysteria from hundreds of fans gathered outside.

The brief appearance overshadowed Bell’s party as hordes of teenage girls raced after the departing vehicle, with Bieber’s driver slowing down so the Canadian star, who was seated in the rear, could open the sliding door and greet them.

Reaching out one of his heavily tattooed arms, Bieber looked genuinely pleased as they fell over each other to snap photos and shake his hand.

In a taunting post added to his Instagram account later that night,the Baby singer posted a shot of Bell watching the van pass by from his vantage point on an upstairs balcony, with the caption: ‘Why is @justinbieber sprinter van getting more attention than my album release party'

Bieber eventually deleted the post, but failed to acknowledge Bell in a later message added to his Twitter account, in which he thanked ‘HIS’ fans for their very public show of affection.

The singer, who sported a baseball cap, baggy black T-shirt and jeans for his latest outing, wrote: ‘To all the fans I met at the grove…I love you. #beliebers.’

His manager Scooter Braun – the man responsible for giving him his big break – echoed his sentiment in a separate post after chatting to him on the phone.

‘Just got a call from @justinbieber and he was beyond happy meeting all the kids down at the grove. You all truly make him smile. Good stuff,’ he tweeted on Thursday evening.

The incident comes just days after Bell claimed he had 'nothing against' Bieber, despite an apparent Twitter spat with the star dating back to 2012.

The former Nickelodeon regular initially claimed his account had been hacked, but further tweets aimed at Bieber appeared on his Twitter feed in 2013, when he uncharitably compared him to Vanilla Ice and rapper turned actor Mark Wahlberg, AKA Marky Mark.

'I hear marky mark is really upset that @Justinbieber stole his look! The 90's called Justin. They want it back,' he mocked last March.

There were further disparaging messages about the singers height, with one reading: 'Justin Bieber is 5'6" now I understand why you guys love him!! He's the same height as all you 12 year olds! I totally get it now sorry guys.'

He also appears to 'diss' Bieber in an Instagram video with fellow singer Aaron Carter.

On Thursday Drake, who found fame on TV show Drake & Josh, looked dapper in a smart blue suit after arriving for the launch, his hair neatly styled into a quiff. 

Drake was joined at the bash by his former two cast-mates from the show, Miranda Cosgrove, 20, and 27-year-old Josh Peck – who was almost unrecognisable as the rotund, eccentric and unpopular teenager he played during its three year run.

Over the weekend Bieber treated fans to a surprise appearance at Coachella, where he joined Chance The Rapper onstage during day three of the annual music and arts festival.

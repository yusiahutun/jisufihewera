Newlywed mother, 47, hanged herself after a day of drinking

Tragic: Mother-of-three Dawn Fowler, 47, was said to be 'happy' after returning from a dream honeymoon with her new husband Craig. But after a night of drinking she was found hanged in her garden in Cheshire

A newlywed mother-of-three who suffered from mood swings hanged herself in her garden after spending an afternoon drinking at a village pub, an inquest heard.

Mother-of-three Dawn Fowler was said to be 'happy' after returning from a dream honeymoon with her new husband Craig.

But the 47-year-old, whose mood swings were said to be worsened by alcohol, went to bed early after a drinking session and was later found by her husband having hanged herself.  

North Staffordshire Coroner's Court was told the couple had been together for more than five years and had married in August last year - almost three months before her death on October 29 last year.

Accounts manager Mr Fowler said his wife, who worked as a forage specialist in the farming industry, had been 'like a best friend' to him.

He described how they had been 'so happy' as they headed on their honeymoon to Thailand. Mrs Fowler had three children from previous relationships.

But he revealed she had previously made an attempt to harm herself and her mood swings tended to be worse when she had been drinking.

On the day she died, they had spent several hours at The Offley Arms near their semi-detached £200,000 home in Madeley, Cheshire, having lunch and drinking with a couple of friends.

'Dawn had consumed about five or six large white wines,' he said.

When he tried to get her to leave the pub and they had a minor argument before walking home to their bungalow.

Mrs Fowler went to bed at around 7pm and fell asleep, briefly rising later on to go to the bathroom. It was around 10.30pm when she was discovered in the garden.

Accounts manager Craig Fowler (pictured) said his wife had been 'like a best friend' to him. Mrs Fowler who suffered from mood swings, went to bed early after a drinking session and was later found by her husband having hanged herself

Her husband said he had stayed up to watch Match of the Day and then went to the bedroom to find she was no longer there.

He spotted the French doors were wide open at their home and went out to investigate and found her.

Mr Fowler called 999 and started CPR as paramedics were on their way. But he was unable to save her and she was pronounced dead at the scene.

A post-mortem examination found the cause of death was asphyxiation due to strangulation.

Tests also showed she had so much alcohol in her system that she would have been nearly three times over the drink-drive limit.

Mrs Fowler's son, Matthew Bromfield, described her as a 'bubbly' person who was 'liked by everybody and had numerous friends'.

North Staffordshire deputy coroner said there was insufficient evidence to prove she had intended to kill herself on the night in question. He said her actions may have been influenced by having so much alcohol in her system and he concluded she died as a result of an accident

But he said did not get on with her new husband and claimed Mr Fowler was trying to 'control' her.

Mr Bromfield said: 'My mother had never expressed feelings of suicide before.'

The inquest heard how Mrs Fowler had mentioned her low moods to her GP several times over the years. But she didn't want to receive counselling or be prescribed medication.

North Staffordshire deputy coroner Anthony Curzon said there was insufficient evidence to prove she had intended to kill herself on the night in question.

He said her actions may have been influenced by having so much alcohol in her system and he concluded she died as a result of an accident.

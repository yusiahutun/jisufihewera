Shop Spring 2024 Transitional Fashion From Mint Velvet

All products and services featured are independently chosen by editors. However, StyleCaster may receive a commission on orders placed through its retail links, and the retailer may receive certain auditable data for accounting purposes.

Even though we’re still shivering and constantly checking the forecast for snow, we’re getting a bit tired of our boring winter wardrobe. Maybe it’s the rise of the elaborate “Mob Wife” trend and the end of “Clean Girl” as we know it, but we’re currently lusting after bright colors and whimsical details — which is why we’re already shopping for spring clothing at Mint Velvet.

The UK-based womenswear brand, which was founded in 2009, describes its style as “Relaxed Glamour.” Everything it sells is meant to be luxurious, effortless, and fun, complete with playful prints and easy shapes you won’t mind slipping on morning, noon, or night. Naturally, Mint Velvet brought the same vibe to its newest clothing and accessories, releasing ruffled button-downs, eye-catching knits, and relaxed trousers as part of its spring 2024 drop.

Ahead, we’re including our favorite Mint Velvet items, which you could no doubt wear and love now, but will also enjoy rocking months down the road. If your fashion goals include comfort, versatility, and small touches that make each item feel special, these pieces are definitely for you.

  • Pink Ruffle Detail Shirt

    When we said our closet could use a boost, we meant it. This pink ruffled shirt is right in line with the top trends of 2024, and since it’s already a statement piece, it won’t require much else to create a standout outfit. Wear it with your favorite pair of jeans for an easy yet fashion-forward look.

  • Navy Longline Scarf Coat

    We’re already big fans of the scarf coat trend, and if you’re in the market for outerwear that will keep you warm but isn’t as boring as a bulky puffer, this design is it. We’re especially fond of the white trim included in Mint Velvet’s navy option, which makes it look way more expensive than its $409 price tag.

  • Taupe Denim Jumpsuit

    We’d like to blame our love of easy outfits on the winter chill, but to be honest, throw-on-and-go is kind of our vibe all year long. But just because it’s quick to slip on doesn’t mean it has to be sweats. This denim jumpsuit is the perfect one-and-done outfit no matter the temperature, and since it’s a neutral base, you can always spice it up with accessories and footwear.

  • Silver Ballet Pumps

    If you’re wondering whether it’s too late to hop on the ballet flats bandwagon, it’s not. The shoe style was all over the Spring 2024 runways along with metallic footwear, which means this silver square-toe option is a great way to nail two trends at once.

  • Cream Pleated Shirt

    If you invest in one Mint Velvet item this season, make it this pleated shirt. The design is so unique and interesting, yet somehow versatile at the same time. You can get away with wearing it to the office, date night dinners, special events, and whatever else you have on your schedule.

  • Cream Loose Knit Jumper

    It is a sweater? A crisp white shirt? Actually, this design appears to be both, and between the unexpected mix of materials and sweet bow details on the sleeves, it’s bound to earn you a ton of compliments and questions about where you got it.

  • Khaki Jacquard Wide Trousers

    Some days, it feels especially hard to peel off our pajamas and pull on stiff jeans. That’s why we were immediately drawn to these printed trousers and its matching top: they’re as soft and cozy as sweats, but look a lot more elevated.

  • Green Ruffle Maxi Dress

    When it comes to transitional pieces, this long ruffled dress reigns supreme. During the colder months, you can style it with tights and boots, but once it warms up a bit, it’ll pair nicely with sneakers or sandals.

  • White Utility Jumper

    Although your drawers might be packed with sweaters, you should probably make room for one more. The utility strap details on this ribbed white design make it stand out from the pack, and it’s the perfect neutral layer to balance out the colorful floral skirts or striped shorts we tend to reach for during the warmer months.

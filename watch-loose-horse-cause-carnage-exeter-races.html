Watch loose horse cause chaos and try to headbutt two others moments after dumping jockey to the gro

THIS is the wild moment an out-of-control horse dumped his jockey to the ground before trying to headbutt two others.

Camprond, who was hugely fancied for the Coral Cup at this year's Cheltenham Festival, went all over the place on his debut over fences at Exeter on Monday.

Trained by Philip Hobbs and owned by legendary billionaire JP McManus, Aidan Coleman stood no chance when the six-year-old gelding sent him flying at the first of 15 fences.

The horse looked to be approaching the flight well before veering sharply right.

Coleman was flung to the left and hit the ground feet-first before his head crashed into the turf.

Camprond jumped the second better but at the third cut in front of 250-1 shot Merchant in Milan.

That horse took the fence at a 45-degree angle and almost forced jockey Charlie Price out the saddle.

The two then did a bit of head-butting before Camprond raced right in front of eventual winner McFabulous.

Laurence Fox's controversial opinions, vaccine comments and massive net worth

Actor, singer-songwriter and controversial character Laurence Fox has made many extreme statements on Covid vaccines in recent times.

Amidst stints of acting and attempting to build a singing career, Laurence ran for London Mayor in 2021, losing with just 1.8% of the vote.

The actor launched a new political party called The Reclaim Party, claiming that his three aims were to defend freedom of speech, reform publicly funded institutions and to celebrate British history.

Here's everything you need to know about Laurence Fox's comments as well as his career and relationship history.

Laurence Fox has spoken out several times on his anti-vaccination views. Recently he posted a picture of himself wearing a t-shirt which read: "No vaccine needed, I have an immune system," captioning his post "I really like my new t.shirt."

The actor-turned-politician also said he would remove his children from school because of his opposition to the Government’s Covid vaccination programme.

He said: "Every parent who loves their children should resist this insanity,’

"I will not be sending my kids back to school. I will educate my kids at home from now on. The rushed vaccination of children, for no reason whatsoever, shows how deeply morally corrupt this regime has become."

What were his comments on unvaccinated Strictly dancers?

More recently after reports of three new Strictly Come Dancing stars refusing to be jabbed, Laurence once again made harsh statements.

He tweeted saying: : "2 professional dancers from @BBCStrictly refuse to have @Covid vaccine. Sack them and replace them with jabbed dancers," adding: "Would you apply the same rules to HIV positive dancers?."

His post has outraged many, causing the Sexual Health Terrence Higgins Trust to respond that: "This is not comparable — HIV is not passed on by dancing. "

The trust clarified that HIV is not "passed on by spitting, sneezing or coughing, kissing or general social contact," and that people on effective treatment "can live long and healthy lives."

Laurence has said he would never date a woman under the age of 35 - the reason being they are "too woke."

Speaking with the Delingpod podcast, he said women under 35 are "primed to believe they are victims," and many are "absolutely bonkers."

He revealed he'd broken up with an ex - whose identity is not clear- because she praised a Gillette advert which highlighted "toxic masculinity."

Then, asked what his ex-girlfriend would think of him discussing their break-up in public, he said: "She will probably sit there and say, 'See I told you he was patriarchal. He's abusing me and I'm offended.'"

Does Laurence Fox have a girlfriend and who has he been in relationships with?

Laurence was married to pop star turned actress Billie Piper from December 2007 to May 2016. The couple divorced with Billie, 35 citing "unreasonable behaviour" as the reason. The couple share custody of their two children - Winston, nine, and Eugene, six.

Recently, Laurence revealed that he'd popped the question to Arabella May Fleetwood Neagle. Sharing the news on Twitter he said: “An announcement. She said yes."

How did Laurence Fox become famous?

The actor rose to fame with his role in Academy Award winning film Gosford Park, after which he went on to to be part of films like Deathwatch, Ultimate Force, Colditz, Elizabeth: The Golden Age and Becoming Jane.

He also had a part in Madonna-directed film W.E. alongside the likes of Abbie Cornish, Andrea Riseborough and Oscar Isaac.

While he has his own acting career, Laurence is known for being part of the famous Fox family acting dynasty. His grandfather is actor and theatrical agent Robin Fox and his dad is actor James Fox.

Besides his siblings, actor Jack Fox and actress Lydia Fox, he also has famous cousins Emilia and Freddie Fox, through his uncle, well-known entertainer Edward Fox.

What is Laurence Fox's net worth

Net Worth Post put Laurence's net worth at $1million (£738,000).

It was reported that he lost a sizeable sum of £10,000 during his mayoral election, losing his deposit following the bitter blow of minuscule votes.

The daughter of former actors Sharon and Larry Palmer has been enjoying living her life out loud, after coming to terms with no longer needing to portray an unrealistic 'PG' version of herself, according to an interview with Billboard magazine. 

'People don't understand. Nickelodeon, Disney Channel... those are corporations,' the True Jackson, VP star said. 'That is a machine and it's a well-oiled machine... Not only when you work with them are you working on their show, but you're working with their brand so you become that identity in which they want you to be.'

Keke went on to say that once she let go of the fear of no longer exuding that brand of purity and perfection, she felt free to truly be herself, which is who the world is getting to know now.

Part of her true self includes a battle with depression and anxiety, fueled by her childhood stardom, which she addresses in her book, according to a review by Cosmopolitan. 

'Being able to say how I feel, that was something that was so hard for me for so long,' she wrote in the book. Now, she has said she has found freedom in unfiltered honesty, and hopes people can relate.

HISTORICAL EVENTS

  • 1853: The Crimean War: The Russian army defeats the Ottomans at the Battle of Sinop.
  • 1924: The first photoelectric cell, a device that converts light into electrical energy, is patented by Albert W. Hull.
  • 1947: The UN General Assembly approves a resolution to partition Palestine into Jewish and Arab states, paving the way for the creation of the state of Israel.
  • 1966: Barbados gains independence from the United Kingdom.
  • 1999: The ExxonMobil merger is announced, creating one of the largest companies in the world.

SCIENTIFIC BREAKTHROUGHS

  • 1954: The first successful kidney transplant is performed by Dr. Joseph E. Murray and Dr. David Hume at Peter Bent Brigham Hospital in Boston.
  • 1999: The Human Genome Project announces that they have completed a preliminary version of the human genome sequence.
  • 2005: The first partial face transplant is performed on Isabelle Dinoire in France.
  • 2012: NASA’s Curiosity rover discovers evidence of an ancient streambed on Mars, suggesting that the planet may have once had conditions suitable for supporting microbial life.
  • 2019: Chinese scientists successfully clone five gene-edited monkeys, using the same technique that was used to create Dolly the sheep.

POLITICAL MILESTONES

  • 1782: The United States and Britain sign the Treaty of Paris, officially ending the American Revolutionary War.
  • 1936: The London Agreement is signed, establishing the framework for the international prosecution of war crimes committed during World War II.
  • 1988: The United Nations Convention Against Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances is signed in Vienna, Austria.
  • 2000: The United States Supreme Court halts the Florida recount, effectively deciding the presidential election in favor of George W. Bush.
  • 2016: Fidel Castro, the former President of Cuba, dies at the age of 90.

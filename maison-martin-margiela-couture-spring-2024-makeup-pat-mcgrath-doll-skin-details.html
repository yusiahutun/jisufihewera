Is This $4 Face Mask the Key to the Margiela Makeup Mystery?

When models hit the runway at Maison Margiela’s Spring 2024 couture show in Paris last week, makeup artists around the world were left stumped. The porcelain-effect, doll-like skin covering the models’ faces looked like a mask made of glass. More videos from backstage revealed the effect wasn’t the result of heaping globs of highlighter or Vaseline, but something else entirely, dreamed and mixed up by the legendary artist Pat McGrath. So what did she use?

The beauty sleuths of TikTok and Instagram have been busy reading comment sections and concocting potions to solve the Margiela makeup mystery. Makeup artist and content creator Ehlie Luna posted a fantastic video that summarizes the current theories about what McGrath used backstage. Kryolan Liquid Glass was the leading guess—and sold out—before that hypothesis was debunked by McGrath herself, in an Instagram post promising she would “show the real product soon” and advising us to “stay tuned.”

Knowing that whatever was on the models’ faces peeled off at the end of the show, the next leading theory was that it was a peel-off face mask diluted with water. Celebrity makeup artist Erin Parsons posted a TikTok that seems to have cracked the case: She was tipped off that the product used backstage looked like Listerine mouthwash, which led her to try the Freeman Renewing Cucumber Peel-Off Gel Facial Mask, a $4 product available at most drugstores. By spraying it on with an airbrush, Parsons was able to achieve a similar look in five coats. “It is almost so simple, but it is—it is perfection,” she said.

Parsons’s video, which currently sits at over two million views, is flooded with comments praising McGrath’s technique. “Pat McGrath’s only competition is herself! Like the way she outdoes herself year after year is incredible!” one commenter wrote. Another couldn’t believe how runway beauty is back: “I haven’t seen so much discourse about fashion or makeup like this in some time. I’m here for it!”

My two cents: I think it’s possible McGrath is launching her own peel-off face mask. It would be the logical next step in the Pat McGrath Labs skincare evolution, following the 2022 release of Divine Skin: Rose 001 The Essence, which is often used backstage in the prep process. We’ll update this post when we learn more from McGrath and her team.

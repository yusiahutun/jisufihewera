Who is David Schultz

What happened to Dave Schultz?

On November 20, 2023, at around 10:30 and 11:00 pm, David Schultz picked up a load of pigs from Eagle Grove, Iowa and was supposed to deliver them to Sac County. Earlier that night, he stopped by his Wall Lake home for a shower and left hurriedly.

On November 21, 2023, Dave's wife, Sarah Schultz, tried reaching him to no avail – she called over 30 times. Fearing for the worst, she called 911 at about 2:30 pm and reported David Schultz from Iowa missing.

Thirty minutes later, she got a call from David’s phone except it wasn’t him on the other side; it was a female officer. She said,

“We found your husband's truck, wallet, and phone. But he's not here."

Where was Dave Schultz's truck found?

Detective Kathryn Stange informed Dateline that Schultz's truck was discovered on Union Avenue in Sac County, Iowa, along 190th Street, after Sarah reported his disappearance. An unsettling detail emerged as investigators noted that the truck was positioned in the opposite direction of David's intended delivery route.

Throughout the investigation process, the Sac County Sheriff's Office, in collaboration with the Iowa Division of Criminal Investigation, conducted extensive searches for David utilizing various resources, including drones, patrol planes, on-foot search teams, cadaver dogs, and ATVs.

Additionally, in the initial stages of the inquiry, the department partnered with the United Cajun Navy, a non-profit organization specializing in disaster relief and search and rescue operations. Although they didn’t find David, they found the body of another missing person in the area, Mark Riesburg.

FAQs about missing persons in USA

  • How long does it take for a missing person to be declared dead in the USA? Several criteria are evaluated to determine whether a missing person may be declared legally dead. The person must have been missing from their residence or home for an extended period, commonly seven years.
  • How long must you wait to report a missing person in the US? While it's recommended to inform your local police department when someone goes missing promptly, certain departments may have a waiting period of up to 24 hours before allowing the filing of an official missing person report, contingent upon the specific circumstances involved.
  • How many people go missing in the US every day? The National Missing and Unidentified Persons System (NamUs) estimates that over 600,000 people go missing yearly in the US. On average, approximately 2,300 people are reported missing daily.
  • What are the chances of finding a missing person? 77% of adults are found within 24 hours, and 87% are located within two days. Only 3% of adults go missing for over a week, and less than 1% for around a month.
  • Where do people go missing the most in the US? California is the state with the most missing persons reported cases.
  • What happens when a missing person is found in the US? When a missing person is reported as found, the police must ensure they're safe; they haven’t been crime victims and won't be harmed again.

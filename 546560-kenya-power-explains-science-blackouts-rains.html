Kenya Power Explains Science Behind Blackouts When It Rains: &quot;Hatuanuangi Stima&quot;

  • Kenya Power disseminated educational content to enlighten customers about the link between rainfall and power interruptions
  • Kenya Power clarified that power disruptions during rainstorms are not deliberate, explaining that adverse weather conditions can lead to transmission line damage
  • Lightning strikes pose another challenge, often causing power outages in regions prone to such weather phenomena

Why do blackouts occur when it rains?

In a post on X, Kenya Power refuted claims it 'unhangs' electricity from the transmission lines, stating that:

"Hatuanuangi stima! Here’s a detailed explanation of the correlation between rain and unplanned power outages."

Kenya Power provided a graphic explanation using photos depicting heavy rains and strong winds, which lead to trees falling on transmission lines.

These trees may break the transmission line, which will lead to blackouts.

In other cases, the trees or branches may trigger protective devices or circuit breakers on the transmission system to switch off electricity.

How lightning causes power blackouts

A blackout may happen when lightning makes contact with power lines, a scenario commonly observed in regions prone to lightning strikes.

Kenya Power has made efforts to mitigate this by installing lightning arrestors in these vulnerable areas.

The abrupt discharge of lightning energy resulting from a direct strike on a transmission line can lead to temporary grid disruptions through line tripping, automatic reclosing, or protection system operations.

According to Kenya Power, lightning strikes add a sudden spike of electric charge to the transmission line, which can add too much voltage to the system and automatically set protective devices to react quickly to avoid damage.

Considering the ongoing heavy rains and floods in various parts of the country Kenyans should take precautionary measures to avoid being caught unprepared by the blackouts.

Kenya Power issues alert on heavy rains

Earlier in April, the Kenya Meteorological Department warned Kenyans to prepare for widespread rainfall and heavy downpours.

Meet Rob McArthur, Amazing Race’s Second Deaf Contestant In Its History

Rob McArthur is one of the contestants on season 35 of The Amazing Race but not an ordinary contestant. He is the second-ever deaf contestant in the show’s history. Rob teamed with his son Corey McArthur on this journey.

One particular exam that truly measures tenacity, humor, and teamwork is The Amazing Race. The program presents viewers with dynamic duos from a variety of backgrounds season after season. The father-son duo of Rob and Corey McArthur stands out among the interesting combinations in the 35th season with a touching tale of bonding, tenacity, and conquering adversity.

Their dynamic gives the difficulties posed by The Amazing Race a unique blend of youthful vigor and expertise. Rob’s job as a teaching assistant at the California School for the Deaf in Riverside, along with Corey’s career goals and love of sports, make them the ideal team for the marathon! In line with Luke Adams, who took part in the 14th season, Rob’s trip is a tremendous global representation of the deaf community in addition to being a personal victory.

Despite the difficulties, he contributed a lot of useful resources to the competitive match. The show’s executive producers and co-creators, Elise Doganieri and Bertram van Munster, created modifications for Rob and Corey to ensure fair competition. The pair was accompanied to all required sites by an American Sign Language (ASL) interpreter, underscoring the show’s dedication to inclusivity and highlighting the strengths of diversity.

Rob and Corey also shared in an IG post that Watching the seasons Luke and Margie raced inspired them to join the show.

By Week 11, they were among the final 3 teams who were still in the competition. the others were Joel Strasser & Garrett Smith and Greg Franklin & John Franklin.

Rob McArthur Wife

Rob McArthur is married to his wife April McArthur, also deaf. It is not clear when they married but they are still happily married to each other as of 2023.

Since January 2023, April has served as the new superintendent of the California School for the Deaf. As the eighth site superintendent in the institution’s history, she will guide it in achieving its goal of fostering student success while upholding its essential principles.

April is not new to CSDR; this is not her first role as Superintendent. Since August of 2022, she has served as the Alternative Curriculum Education (ACE) principal. April was an elementary principal and the ASL-English bilingual director of the Washington School for the Deaf/Washington State Center for Deaf and Hard of Hearing Youth before taking on the role of ACE principal on campus. There, she trained and consulted legislators around the state of Washington as well as staff members of school districts and their families.

April graduated with a master’s degree in Teaching and Learning: ASL and English Bilingual Education from the University of California, San Diego, and an administrator’s credential from Washington State University in Vancouver. She attended California State University, Northridge to earn her undergraduate degree.

Besides Corey, April and Rob also have a son named Kellan McArthur. You can find Kellan on IG here. He attended the Martin Luther King High School.

April has supported her husband and son’s journey on Facebook as she wrote, “Guess what!?! I am thrilled to share that Rob and Corey had the incredible opportunity to participate in the Amazing Race! Deaf Dad/CODA Son duo-in the red shirts! You can follow them on Instagram @robandcorey where we will post regular information about their season. The show airs on Sept 27th @ 9:30 pm on CBS and Paramount+! SO excited!!.”

Rob McArthur Age

Rob McArthur was born on 13 January 1975. Hence, he is 48 years old as of 2023.

Rob McArthur Job

Rob McArthur deals with the difficulties of having a hearing impairment while working as an assistant football coach and teacher’s assistant at the California School for the Deaf in Riverside. His school announced his appearance on The Amazing Race,

According to his LinkedIn, he is a coach at Washington School for the Deaf. They wrote, “Rob McArthur, who is deaf and a teacher’s aide and assistant football coach at the school, is featured with his 25-year-old son, Corey McArthur, a hearing child of deaf adults, a deaf school news release states.”

The Amazing Race Kickoff Premiere Watch Party was scheduled on Wednesday, September 27, 2023, at 6:30 p.m. in the Multi-Purpose Activity Center on campus. The public was invited. The deaf school observed Deaf Community Cultural Week from Monday, September 25, through Friday, September 29, in honor of the program. The World Federation of the Deaf’s 2023 theme, “An Amazing World Where Deaf People Everywhere Can Sign Anywhere,” served as the inspiration for the celebration.

Meanwhile, the deaf school rooted for the McArthur team on TV.

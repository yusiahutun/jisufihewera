Chelsea edge out Newcastle as Callum Hudson-Odoi shines but Tammy Abraham flops

For more than hour Chelsea appeared destined for a fruitless afternoon as a string of second half chances were wasted.

But with Callum Hudson-Odoi refusing to accept they could not break down Steve Bruce’s men, the Blues finally struck gold.

The England man’s neat pass set up Marcos Alonso’s decisive strike midway the period. The fact the scorer used to play for Sunderland only compounded the Geordies’ misery.

Three Good:

Callum Hudson-Odoi - Only his second Premier League start since his recovery from last season’s Achilles injury but you would never have guessed it.

Easily the most dangerous player on the pitch yesterday, the England ace demonstrated why he is both the present and future of Frank Lampard’s Blue revolution

His lengthy lay-off has done nothing to diminish his lightning pace or ability to dazzle opponents and delight the home faithful in equal measure.

Jorginho - The often maligned holding Chelsea midfielder is the perfect general in the centre of the park for this fresh-faced Lampard outfit.

In addition to rarely losing possession, Jorginho also organises and instructs those around him.

He’s the equivalent of an on-pitch manager and ideal for all the youngsters around him.

Allan Saint-Maximin - The £16m summer signing from Nice produced some of the best moments for the Geordies.

Strong, fast, and with no little skill to boot, the Gallic winger was a genuine live-wire on the left flank.

Unfortunately, his team-mates were not quite on the same wizardry wavelength – and much of his good work went to waste.

Two Bad:

Tammy Abraham - On fire before the international break, England’s new international had one of those afternoons when nothing quite went right.

Not through any lack of effort but more misfortune. In the space of 11 second half-minutes he could have scored a hat-trick of headers.

But the first effort smacked again the bar – while the other two were just off target. Then his close-range strike was denied by an incredible last ditch Miguel Yedlin clearance.

Martin Dubravka - Feel a bit harsh being too critical of the Magpies keeper given he made one sensational save that looked to have earned the visitors on course for a point.

But having done brilliantly to deny Christian Pulisic – with a reflex stop from the American’s point blank effort – his afternoon suddenly turned sour.

Yes, Marcos Alonso’s angled first-time drive from the left was struck with power but the Slovakia stopper should have done better as it slipped past his left hand and into the corner.

Meaning of We Werent Born To Follow by Bon Jovi

The Bon Jovi song "We Weren't Born To Follow" is an anthem that speaks to the indomitable spirit within each of us. The overall theme of the song encourages listeners to rise above adversity, challenge the status quo, and stand up for what they believe in. It is a call to action, an invitation to embrace individuality, and a reminder that we have the power to shape our own destinies. Bon Jovi's lyrics explore various aspects of this overarching theme, delving into the depths of human experience and emotion, and weaving together a tapestry of resilience, hope, and determination.The opening lyrics immediately set the stage for the powerful message of the song. "This one goes out to the man who mines for miracles, This one goes out to the ones in need." These lines highlight the heroism in everyday individuals who face extraordinary challenges. It speaks to the unwavering strength and unwavering faith necessary to persevere and find hope in even the most difficult circumstances."This road was paved by the hopeless and the hungry, This road was paved by the winds of change, Walking beside the guilty and the innocent." These lyrics reflect the collective journey we all embark on. They suggest that the path to success and personal growth is not limited to a select few, but rather, it is a path that is shared by all -- the hopeful and the desperate, the sinners and the saints. It emphasizes the importance of unity and empathy, reminding us that we are all bound together on this journey called life.One standout lyric in the song is "This one's about anyone who does it differently, This one's about the one who curses and spits." These lines challenge societal norms and celebrate individuality. They deliver the message that it is essential to embrace our differences, step outside of our comfort zones, and challenge the conventional wisdom that holds us back. By doing so, we can create positive change, inspire others, and leave a lasting impact on the world."We weren't born to follow, Come on and get up off your knees, When life is a bitter pill to swallow, You gotta hold onto what you believe." These lines capture the essence of the song's theme and serve as a powerful call to action. They encourage listeners not to succumb to the challenges and hardships they face but to rise above them. The lyrics convey the importance of perseverance, resilience, and the strength to stand firm in one's convictions.Another significant lyric is "Believe that the sun will shine tomorrow, And that your saints and sinners bleed." This line seamlessly weaves together hope and vulnerability. It reminds us that even in the darkest times, there is always a glimmer of hope, a ray of light that can guide us forward. It also speaks to the shared human experience, acknowledging that no one is infallible and that we all have our flaws and shortcomings.In conclusion, "We Weren't Born To Follow" is a powerful anthem that encourages listeners to rise above adversity, embrace individuality, and stand up for what they believe in. The song explores various themes, including the importance of unity, empathy, perseverance, and hope. Its lyrics delve into the depths of human experiences and emotions, weaving together a narrative of strength, courage, and resilience. It serves as a reminder that each of us has the power to shape our own destinies and make a positive impact on the world. So, let us all rise up, hold onto our beliefs, and pave our own paths to a brighter tomorrow.

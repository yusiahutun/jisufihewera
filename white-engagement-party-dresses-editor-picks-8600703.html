I Found My Engagement Party Dress, but These 8 Picks Were Top Contenders

It’s been three weeks since I said “I do,” and it’s officially time to celebrate. My mom is planning the soirée of my dreams back home in California, and my New York City bestie is snagging all of the balloons and streamers for a TriState-area affair. But even with all of their arrangements and organization, there’s still one thing they can’t do: choose my engagement party dress; that’s up to me.

After tons of research, I landed on Showpo’s Stephane corset mini dress — but there were so many top contenders. Soon-to-be brides already have a long to-do list, so I’m sharing my favorite pearly-white picks below to make choosing an engagement party dress one less thing to worry about.

Editor-Approved Engagement Party Dresses

Dress the Population Tiffany One-Shoulder Midi Dress

This Dress the Population piece stood out to me due to its classic appearance; it has a timeless one-shoulder silhouette with a draped, bow-like detail. An elegant back slit and body-hugging fit are flattering on the wearer, plus, you can’t go wrong with a classy midi length. I envision wearing this pick to a ballroom celebration with beige, stiletto heels. For a shorter alternative, consider the brand’s mini version. 

Wdirara Mesh Polka-Dot Long-Sleeve Dress

If you’re looking for something more unique, consider Wdirara’s polka-dot mini dress. The mesh, puff sleeves are fun and airy, while the sweetheart neckline and ribbed details compliment many bust sizes. The flared A-line hem creates a flowy, princess-like experience, while a back zipper keeps things practical. This is a great option for outdoor parties thanks to its short length and lightweight material. 

Petal and Pup Lauren Lace Overlay Dress

I’ve had my eye on this Petal and Pup dress since before I got engaged. The two-piece illusion and slim fit offer a mature and sexy option, while a lace overlay, ruffled sleeves, and flared hemline add a quirky appeal. This is the ideal midi dress for garden parties and darling engagement gatherings, such as tea parties. 

Merokeety V-Neck Floral Lace Mini Dress

If you’re having your engagement party in a brewery or a backyard, think about snagging this Merokeety dress. It’s more casual than the other options, but just as chic; eyelash lace, a V-neckline, high waist, and scalloped edges work together seamlessly for an easy-breezy look. Wear it with silver rhinestone boots for a country vibe, or ribbon wedges to dress it up even more. If you like the style but wish it was longer, then check out this similar style from Mermaid Closet.  

The key to choosing your dream party dress is considering where your engagement gathering will be to ensure you match the aesthetic, as well as securing something you feel beautiful and comfortable in. These picks aren’t only great for engagement celebrations but work just as well for bridal parties, engagement photos, and everything in between — you may even decide to wear it post wedding to date night, family events, and beyond.

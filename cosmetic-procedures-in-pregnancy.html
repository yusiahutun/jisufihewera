Can you have Botox when pregnant? Cosmetic procedures in pregnancy

Those routinely getting aesthetic treatments – be they dermal fillers, laser facials or peels – may not know whether to go cold turkey on the procedures after finding out that they're pregnant. Most of us assume that Botox would be a bad idea, but what about skin needling – that should be safe, right? Not necessarily. We asked aesthetic physician Dr. Ravi Jain of the Riverbanks Wellness Clinic what he advises his clients who may be planning a pregnancy.

Are fillers ever safe to use in pregnancy and while breastfeeding?

"There is always a risk with any medical intervention - including dermal fillers. So why take a risk during one of the most precious times in your life? Also, weight and fluid balance can change during pregnancy and so how the fillers may look will change during pregnancy. I advise patients not to have any injectables unless absolutely necessary - i.e. for health reasons."

What about Botox?

"There is no clinical evidence suggesting that having Botox is unsafe in pregnancy or while breastfeeding, but it is sensible practice not to inject a toxin in pregnant women just in case something happens to the baby. Nobody is going to conduct a clinical study on this due to ethical considerations."

How long should you wait post-pregnancy to have Botox?

"As soon as you stop breastfeeding it is okay to start Botox again, but it's best to wait until your skin and hormones have settled down."

Which skin peels are safe in pregnancy?

"Mild superficial peels such as lactic acids and glycolic acid are generally fine, but just be prepared that the skin may react differently to normal in pregnancy due to the hormonal changes."

Can you have facial laser treatments?

"Skin reactions may be unpredictable during pregnancy and the risk of hyperpigmentation is higher, so therefore I would avoid having any laser treatments."

Is microneedling considered safe?

"Needling is safer, as long as shallow needles are being used (such as 0.5mm). You don't want to carry the risk of causing hyperpigmentation by overdoing it either at home or in a clinic."

The bottom line...

As Dr. Jain points out, "usually, women glow and look radiant – and therefore younger when pregnant," which can be a beauty boost enough. "So why bother with anything aesthetic?" he questions.

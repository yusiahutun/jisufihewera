'Catfishing': The new con used by online scammers that can leave their victims broken

'Catfishing' is a term for the process of luring people into false relationships that has become increasingly prevalent with the rise of social media. 

By creating fake profiles on social networking sites, predators can trick people into thinking that they are someone else entirely.

The fabricated life stories and photographs that they cobble together online often contain the experiences, friends, resumés and job titles that they wish were their own.

Nev Shulman, right, and Max Joseph appearing on MTV's Catfish show

Social media and dating accounts offer a window into the life these scammers want the world to see, but the real people behind the façade are often totally different.

The emergence of these elaborate social schemes was brought to light in the 2010 documentary 'Catfish,' in which 28-year-old Nev Schulman fell in love with a gorgeous young woman's Facebook profile.

He spoke to her via messages and over the phone - but the person on the other end of his electronic devices turned out to belong to a middle-aged wife and mother. 

And although it can be devastating for its victims, the process is not necessarily illegal.

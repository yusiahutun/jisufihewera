13 Easy Ideas for What to Wear to a Country Concert

Wearing flannel to a country concert isn't mandatory, but it's highly recommended. If plain gingham isn't your go-to, but you're headed to a show and hoping to standout from the crowd or update your look, there's plenty of twists on the expected country fare to be had. According to personal stylist Christina Stein, it's all in the details. 

"You can have fringe on your boots, denim jacket or top, handbag, or a statement belt to wear over your shorts," she said. "The possibilities are endless — and it gives texture and personality to even the simplest look."

Other traditional accessories like cowboy hats and boots work with contemporary pieces, too. 

"They add a little edge to softer silhouettes," Stein said of cowboy boots. "Denim Daisy Dukes and boots are classic pieces to wear, but don't forget that your boots can also be worn with a dress or a romper!" 

Not to mention that beyond the classics are a whole host of twists on patterns, accessories, and fabrics that are perfectly at home at a country show. The secret to updating your look? It's not only in the details but also in keeping the silhouettes contemporary and balanced, and making sure not to verge on costume. Mixing fabrics, embracing a monochromatic look, or throwing in an oversized item are a great start.

Ahead, a few looks to get you started on what to wear to a country concert.

Rock Head-to-Toe Denim

If you've been looking for an occasion that screams denim, a country concert couldn't be any more apropos. Going head-to-toe is especially modern in a looser, baggier silhouette, like with a matching shacket and wider cut jeans. Tawny leather embellishments and accessories give the look a luxe feel and complement deep blue denim shades well.

Try Leather Pants

Want to make your look a little bit country and rock and roll? Leather pants are the perfect combination and give a little extra oomph to that concert tee you're wondering how to style, but leather pants also work well with a denim/chambray shirt. You can still incorporate a sweater if an outdoor venue turns chilly late into the evening.

Accessorize Jean Shorts

They don't need to be Daisy Duke's, but jean shorts are practically a prerequisite for a country show. Long or short, spruce up the classic look with boots, fun accessories, and even your favorite sweatshirt.

Tie on a Bandana

Whether it's as a neckerchief, headband, belt, or other styling, a bandana instantly gives your ensemble a relaxed and casual vibe, plus it's perfect for tying your hair back or dabbing away sweat after a night of dancing along to music.

Wear Lots of Leather

An oversized leather jacket is a great start, but leather boots and a bag signal you mean business at a concert and plan to rock out all night long!

Embrace Folk Style

There's plenty of Americana to explore through fashion, so take some inspo from the resurgence in folk and regional styles, like a southwestern-inspired jacket or a crochet top and accessories.

Go for a Baseball Cap

If a band tee isn't quite your style, you can support your favorite act wearing a baseball cap or a hat that celebrates the local sports team. Pair the look with a vintage oversized leather jacket of a maxidress for extra comfort.

Country-fy a Sundress

A sundress will keep you cool, breezy, and comfortable during a warm outdoor show, but the key to nailing this look is in the accessories. Cowboy boots aren't necessary, but they're strongly encouraged, as well as a crossbodywith fringe detailing that adds texture.

Get Creative with Plaid

Whether it's a shirt, shacket, or dress, you can't go wrong with a hint of plaid for a country show, but you can also take it to the next level. Consider a two piece monochromatic plaid ensemble, a maxidress or skirt, or even mismatched plaids and checks for an ultra-modern take on the expected pattern.

Pull Out a Jean Jacket

Your favorite oversized or torn up jean jacket is ready for its moment in the sun. A vintage or distressed style with patches or embroidery is ideal, paired with a maxidress, motorcycle or cowboy boots, and easy waves.

Sneak in Some Fringe Details

Fringe doesn't have to be expected. A mini, boots, jacket, or bag with even a hint of fringed detailing will do the trick and help your look appear layered and thoughtful.

Throw on a Pair of Cowboy Boots

As cliché as it may sound, what is a country concert without cowboy boots? Few items pair just as well with a sundress as they do shorts, jeans, and almost every other possible ensemble.

Top the Look Off with a Cowboy Hat

Houston native Beyoncé wears one at her shows, and could there be any better precedent? Avoid looking costume-y by pairing your cowboy hat with a skirt (mini or otherwise) and at least one oversized item (like a jacket) for a contemporary silhouette.
